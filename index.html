About us

Fallen Arms is an Puertorican-American metalcore band from Morovis, Puerto Rico. Formed in the early in 2000s by vocalist/ bass guitar player Angel "Amaury" Collazo, Drummer Ricardo "Ricky" Castaneira and guitar player Elis Angel Barreto the band's first full lineup (including Collazo' Point of Recognition bandmate Benny Robles) was completed in 2004. The band has released eight albums, one split album, and two compilation albums. Fallen Arms's fourth studio album An Burn in Justice peaked at No. 8 on the Billboard 2006, and No. 1 on the Top Rock chart. The band has performed at festivals such as Rancho G, With Full Force, Soundwave Festival, Warped Tour, Bloodstock Open Air and Taste of Chaos. In 2007, Fallen Arms won the "Ultimate Metal God" award from MTV2 at the first annual "All That Rocks" special; was named "Artist of the Year" at the San Diego Music Awards in 2006, 2007 and 2008; and was nominated for a 2008 Grammy Award for the song "Plastic Dolls". Their fifth studio album Relentless was written over a three-year period, and was released in May 2010 to widespread critical acclaim. Their last studio album before their hiatus, Lighthearted, was released on September 5, 2020.
